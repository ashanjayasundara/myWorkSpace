Digital Pulz PAC System

The DigitalPulz HTML5 Medical Viewer is a powerful collection of JavaScript libraries and RESTful Web Services that provide a platform-independent web application for displaying DICOM images from any PACS. This fully-customizable web application is perfect for any developer who needs fast, lightweight DICOM viewing without sacrificing any features that healthcare professionals need.

There are several researches have been carried out throughout the world regarding Health Information System (HIS). In Sri Lanka health organizations especially Government hospitals are heavily dependent on documentation starting at the out-patient, admission record and extending up to disease notification and national health statistics etc. Apart from that all the Staff and management records are maintained in bulky paper work .

As a solution for this problem several teams of Undergraduates of SLIIT has been developing Health Information System (HIS) for Government hospitals in Sri Lanka within last two years. Most of the core modules are now developed and properly working in this HIS framework.

HHIRMS (Hospital Health Information Management System) is another functioning open source medical record system which was developed by ICTA in Sri Lanka. This was initially developed for Dompe District hospital in Western Province of Sri Lanka, This system is now functioning in 19 Government hospitals in Sri Lanka.

MyFreePACS is a PACS (Picture Archiving and Communication System) was developed for Childrenâ€™s Hospital Seattle to provide an easy-to-use, easy method for viewing Radiology images from anywhere on a hospital network, or remotely over VPN. This uses DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine) server to receive and store medical images. This web client which is a web page that uses for viewing and manipulation of images.

PacsOneServer is another well-developed freely available PAC server. It uses open source MySQL database and Apache web servers. This can be used in many software operating systems like Linux, Mac OS X, Windows NT, Windows 20009 etc. PacsOneServer comprised with several functionalities such as more Transfer syntaxes supported, Automatic Image Routing, System Journal Log Upload DICOM Images from Web Browsers, and as well as HL7 interfaces.


There have been a number of studies carried out to overcome the storing and receiving of medical images in Health Information Systems. Even though there are several system developed as a solution for the above problem none of these systems are not capable of catering the Sri Lankan context.

Most of the solutions provided were not capable of handling and maintaining those systems in the Sri Lankan Government hospitals due to lack of technical knowledge of staff and as well as requirement of advanced equipment for these systems. In Government hospitals in Sri Lanka require more user friendly and easy-to-handle system for their busy daily routine and to maintain.

In the HHIMS developed by ICTA for Dompe hospital in Sri Lanka compromised with several user issues as well as lack of major modules. RIS is one of the major module that has not been developed in this HHIMS.

This system does not contain any method to handle medical images or storing the original images. That is a major drawback of this HHISM system.









PACS can impact on clinical work practices in 3 major ways. PACS improve efficiency of work practices. Quick image availability can impact on clinical decision making and also it can change communication practices between doctor and radiology.

PACS have several advantages for Doctors as well as for patients. Most of the time patients come to clinics forgetting their medical images (X-ray, CT or any other medical images.) Damaging to films is another major concern of hardcopies of medical images. With the time some medical images are tend to disappear from the hard copied or getting blurred or deformed. In that case medical practitioner faces difficulty in examine the medical image and giving proper prescription for the patient. Without having proper communication between various department and personnel it would be difficult to get correct medical image without delaying.

The cost for printing these medical images are now major concern. By lowering the cost for hardcopies will make health sector more beneficial for both Government as well as for patients. Each year considerably large amount of space is wasted to store these copies of medical images. Doctors face difficulties in diagnosing or prescription when not having clear, misplaced, unavailable or damaged medical images. Having paper based record on medical images doctors spend more time reading those reports one by one and hence spend large amount of time for diagnosing and identifying past conditions with patients.

As a solution for this condition should have an automated medical image system in Sri Lanka health sector.
